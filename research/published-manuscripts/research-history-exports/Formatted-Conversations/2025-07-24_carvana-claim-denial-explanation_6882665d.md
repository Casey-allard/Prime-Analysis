My friend bought a car from Carvana and Carvana said they should take it to inspection so they will fix the alignment after it was purchased - after taking it to the dealer Carvana is not denying the claim by the dealer. How are they allowed to sell a car and deny the claim to fix it after the fact?

### Assistant (2025-07-24 12:59:10)

Carvana’s ability to deny a claim for post-sale repairs like alignment—even after telling the buyer to take it in—usually hinges on a combination of legal wiggle room in their **sales agreement**, **limited warranty terms**, and the **nature of the issue diagnosed by the dealer**.

Here’s how they are typically able to get away with it, and what your friend can do:

---

### 🚨 Why Carvana Might Legally Deny the Claim

1. **Warranty Coverage Loopholes**
   - Most Carvana vehicles come with a **100-day / 4,189-mile limited warranty** via a third-party like SilverRock.
   - **Alignment issues** are often considered a “wear and tear” or maintenance item, **not covered** unless caused by a covered mechanical failure (like a bad suspension part).
   - So even if something feels "wrong," if it's chalked up to regular usage or pre-existing wear, they’ll deny it.

2. **Dealer's Inspection ≠ Covered Claim**
   - If the **dealer’s report** doesn’t specifically tie the alignment issue to a **covered part failure**, Carvana can say: "Not our responsibility."
   - Example: Dealer says, “car needs an alignment.” Carvana says, “that’s not a warrantied repair.”

3. **Verbal vs. Written Statements**
   - Even if a Carvana rep *said* to go get it looked at and they’d fix it, unless that promise was in **writing**, they’re likely to deny responsibility.

---

### 🔍 What Your Friend Can Do About It

1. **Get the Inspection Report in Writing**
   - Make sure the dealer’s report **clearly states the cause** of the alignment issue.
   - If it's due to a mechanical failure (e.g., bent control arm, worn bushing, tie rod damage), **that may be covered**.

2. **Refile or Escalate the Claim**
   - Submit a new claim or appeal the denial, referencing **specific parts** tied to the alignment.
   - Insist on speaking with a supervisor or **file a complaint through SilverRock**, if that's the warranty provider.

3. **File a BBB or State Attorney General Complaint**
   - Carvana is sensitive to **BBB and regulatory complaints**. If the situation feels deceptive, this adds pressure.
   - Your friend can file complaints with:
     - [Better Business Bureau](https://www.bbb.org)
     - Your **state Attorney General**
     - [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB)](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/)

4. **Mention Lemon Law / Consumer Protection**
   - If the issue occurred within a few days and affects drivability/safety, **state lemon laws** or **used car buyer protections** (like California’s implied warranty) could apply.
   - Carvana may act to avoid legal escalation or bad press.

---

### ✍️ Summary

Carvana can deny the repair if:
- The issue is not explicitly covered by their warranty.
- The inspection doesn't clearly link it to a covered mechanical failure.
- They never formally promised to pay for it in writing.

But your friend isn’t out of options. If they get documentation that the problem stems from a covered defect, **persistently escalating the claim** or filing formal complaints **often gets results**.
