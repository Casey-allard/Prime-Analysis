---

## The core problem: cellulose armor

Corn kernels are wrapped in a tough outer shell called the **pericarp**, which is mostly **cellulose**.

- Humans **cannot digest cellulose**.
- We lack the enzyme **cellulase**.
- Ruminants (cows, goats) outsource this job to gut microbes. You do not.

So the shell survives your digestive tract mostly intact, waving at you on the way out.

---

## Important clarification most people miss

You **do digest corn**. Just not all of it.

What gets absorbed just fine:
- **Starch** (glucose)
- **Sugars**
- **Proteins**
- **Fats**
- **Vitamins and minerals**

What doesn’t:
- The **outer hull**

That hull traps some nutrients inside, especially if the corn wasn’t well-chewed. Swallow kernels whole and congratulations, you skipped a step evolution expected you to do manually.

---

## Why corn seems worse than other vegetables

Other plant foods:
- Have thinner cell walls
- Break down more easily with cooking
- Release nutrients faster

Corn:
- Has **thick cellulose walls**
- Is often eaten quickly, minimally chewed
- Is commonly processed in ways that preserve kernel structure

So it’s not special. It’s just **structurally stubborn**.

---

## Fun anthropological footnote

Indigenous cultures figured this out centuries ago.

They used **nixtamalization** (soaking corn in an alkaline solution like lime water):
- Breaks down the hull
- Makes nutrients bioavailable
- Prevents deficiencies like pellagra

Modern humans skipped that wisdom and replaced it with corn syrup and vibes.

---

## Bottom line

- Your body processes **most** of corn.
- The visible leftovers are **cellulose**, not failure.
- Chew better, cook thoroughly, or process it traditionally.
- Or accept that corn is the one food that insists on reminding you of basic biochemistry after dinner.

Evolution giveth. Corn mocketh.

- people will act altruistically long-term  
- power will self-limit  
- elites will voluntarily stop extracting value  

is a bedtime story for adults.

Capitalism works better than its rivals because it answers that question *accidentally* better than most.

---

## Why capitalism survives (despite being awful)

Capitalism does three brutally effective things:

1. **It harnesses greed instead of suppressing it**
   - “Want more? Fine. Create value someone else wants.”
2. **It decentralizes ambition**
   - You don’t need permission to try.
3. **It creates feedback**
   - Bad ideas usually fail fast. Usually.

Its fatal flaw:
- It **concentrates power over time**
- It turns markets into oligarchies
- It monetizes everything, including damage

Left alone, it eats its own host. History is very clear on this.

---

## Why authoritarian and communal systems fail harder

### Authoritarian systems
- Claim to suppress greed
- Actually **monopolize it**
- Create unaccountable elites
- Collapse under corruption or fear

They don’t remove greed.  
They **remove escape velocity**.

### Pure communal systems
- Assume people will contribute indefinitely without differential reward
- Depend on social pressure and moral purity
- Break the moment scale, anonymity, or hardship appears

They work:
- in small groups
- with shared identity
- under external threat

They do not scale to nations without coercion. Ever.

---

## The system that *might* work long-term

Not a replacement for capitalism.
A **constrained, hybrid parasite** that lives on top of it.

Call it whatever you want. The components matter more than the name.

### 1. Markets for creation, not extraction
- Competitive markets for **new value**
- Hard limits on rent-seeking
- Aggressive antitrust, permanently

Greed is allowed to build.
Not allowed to entrench.

### 2. Automatic redistribution, not moralized charity
- Universal basic floor (income, healthcare, housing access)
- No means testing theater
- No “deserving poor” nonsense

This:
- stabilizes labor
- reduces fear-based compliance
- increases risk-taking and innovation

People work better when they’re not one medical bill from ruin. Shocking, I know.

### 3. Ownership spread, not wage-only labor
This is the part almost everyone skips.

- Employee ownership
- Profit-sharing
- Public equity stakes in critical infrastructure
- Sovereign wealth funds that actually benefit citizens

You motivate labor by letting them **own the upside**, not just survive the downside.

### 4. Power expiration dates
Greed plus permanence equals exploitation.

- Term limits everywhere power accumulates
- Forced divestment thresholds
- No “too big to fail,” only “too big to exist”

If capital has to move, it behaves. If it squats, it rots.

---

## The uncomfortable answer to your core question

> How do you motivate the labor class while sharing prosperity?

You don’t motivate them with slogans.
You motivate them with **agency, upside, and security**.

- Agency: real choices
- Upside: ownership, not crumbs
- Security: a floor that cannot be pulled out from under them

People are not lazy.
They are **risk-averse under predation**.

Remove predation, effort returns.

---

## The bleak but honest conclusion

There is no system that:
- eliminates greed
- stays fair forever
- runs itself

The best possible system:
- **assumes corruption**
- **limits accumulation**
- **redistributes automatically**
- **lets people fail without being destroyed**

Capitalism is still in the picture.
It just stops being the god.

Not utopia.  
Just survivable.

Which, historically speaking, is a massive upgrade.
