### Understanding Iambic Pentameter

Iambic pentameter is a type of metric line used in traditional English poetry and verse drama. The "iamb" is a metrical foot consisting of an unstressed syllable followed by a stressed syllable (da-DUM). "Pentameter" indicates that a line has five of these feet, making ten syllables in total.

"To BE, or NOT to BE, that IS the QUEStion."

Each pair of syllables (da-DUM) forms one iamb, and this line has five iambs, making it iambic pentameter.
