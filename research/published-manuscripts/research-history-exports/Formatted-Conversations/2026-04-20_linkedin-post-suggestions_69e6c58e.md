- memory of your preferences
- session context accumulation
- ranking or response selection shaped by prior interaction patterns
- system-level adaptation around the model rather than deep retraining of the model itself
- possibly user embeddings, retrieval context, or personalization layers

So, “individualized backprop” in the strict sense is usually not the most likely mechanism. “Individualized behavioral shaping through context, memory, and surrounding infrastructure” is much more plausible.

## The clean distinction

There are three different things people blur together:

| Mechanism | What it is | Most likely? |
|---|---|---|
| **True backprop on the base model** | Updating weights from your specific interactions | Usually not per interaction |
| **Contextual adaptation** | Using conversation history, memory, style, and prior preferences | Very likely |
| **Human skill improvement** | You learning how to elicit better outputs | Also very likely |

So the answer is probably:

## Mostly:
**humanity is getting better at interacting with AI**

## Also:
**AI products are increasingly wrapped in systems that make them feel individually responsive**

That combination creates the illusion of something deeper than either piece alone.

## The game-theoretic version

Each side is adapting to the other.

- Humans learn which prompts produce coherence.
- AI systems learn which interaction patterns keep users engaged and satisfied.
- The platform learns what kind of response style “works” for a given user.
- The user then starts optimizing against that learned behavior.

That is a feedback loop, even without full per-user weight training.

So no, it does not have to be literal individualized backprop for it to feel like mutual shaping. The world is rarely elegant enough to use the coolest mechanism when a cheaper hack gets 80% of the effect. Tragic, but standard.

## My actual judgment

If you’re asking what explains most of the effect you notice in practice:

**1. Better user steering**
**2. Better personalization layers around the model**
**3. Much less likely: direct individualized backprop into core model weights**

That said, from the user’s perspective, the phenomenology can look almost identical. Which is exactly the sort of irritating ambiguity modern AI systems are built on.
