### Assistant (2025-07-30 20:17:10)

The prefix **“pan-”** comes from the Greek **πᾶν (pan)** meaning **“all,” “every,” or “completely.”** When attached to a concept, it indicates that the scope of that concept is universal or all-encompassing.  

So in the case of **pan-determinism**:

- **Determinism** is the idea that every event or state is caused by preceding events in accordance with natural laws—nothing happens without a cause.
- **Pan-determinism** extends that idea to **all phenomena without exception**.  
  - It implies that **everything**—physical, mental, or even seemingly random—**is entirely determined by prior causes**.
  - There is **no room for chance, spontaneity, or free will** in a pan-deterministic framework.

In other words, **pan-determinism = “determinism applied to all things.”**  

---

If you want a comparison with other “pan-” terms:  
- **Panpsychism** → “all things have mind or consciousness.”  
- **Pantheism** → “all is God” or “God is in all things.”  
- **Panopticon** → “seeing all” (pan + opticon).
