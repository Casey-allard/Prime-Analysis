### Assistant (2024-02-28 20:10:33)

The Riemann Zeta function is a complex function that plays a pivotal role in number theory and related fields of mathematics. It is defined in various ways across different regions of the complex plane, but it's most famously represented as an infinite series and as a product over prime numbers, showcasing its deep connections to prime numbers and their distribution.

### Definition

For complex numbers \(s\) with the real part greater than 1 (\(\Re(s) > 1\)), the Riemann Zeta function \(\zeta(s)\) is defined by the infinite series:

\[
\zeta(s) = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^s}
\]

This series converges for \(\Re(s) > 1\), providing a clear and direct link between the Zeta function and the distribution of integers.

### Analytic Continuation

The function can be extended to the rest of the complex plane, except for a simple pole at \(s = 1\), through a process known as analytic continuation. This extended definition allows the Zeta function to be evaluated for complex numbers outside the region where the series converges naturally.

### Euler Product Formula

One of the most remarkable properties of the Zeta function is its connection to prime numbers, elegantly expressed by the Euler product formula:

\[
\zeta(s) = \prod_{p \; \text{prime}} \frac{1}{1 - p^{-s}}
\]

This formula, valid for \(\Re(s) > 1\), shows that the Zeta function is intimately linked to the prime numbers, as the product runs over all prime numbers \(p\). It essentially encodes the fundamental theorem of arithmetic, which states that every positive integer can be uniquely factored into prime numbers.

### Importance

The Riemann Zeta function is central to the field of analytic number theory and has applications in physics, probability theory, and statistics. Its significance, however, is most famously captured by the Riemann Hypothesis, one of the seven Millennium Prize Problems. The hypothesis posits that all non-trivial zeros of the Zeta function have their real part equal to \(1/2\), implying profound consequences for the distribution of prime numbers.

The zeros of the Zeta function, especially those lying on the critical line \(\Re(s) = 1/2\), are of great interest because their distribution is believed to be closely related to the pattern of prime numbers. This deep connection between the Zeta function and prime numbers underscores much of the intrigue and study devoted to this function, making it one of the most studied objects in mathematics.
