## 🧠 What’s *actually* different on desktop
On your phone, voice mode is tightly integrated with the OS. On desktop, it’s… let’s call it “aspirationally seamless.” It depends on permissions, hardware, and the app behaving itself.

---

## 🔧 The usual culprits (in order of likelihood)

### 1. Mic permissions aren’t actually enabled
Your app might *think* it has access. Your OS might disagree.

- **macOS**:  
  System Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone  
  Make sure ChatGPT is enabled  
- Then restart the app (yes, really)

---

### 2. Wrong input device
Desktop loves picking the *worst possible mic*.

- Check your system input device (AirPods, USB mic, etc.)
- Speak and see if input levels move
- If not, you’re talking into the void

---

### 3. You’re not hitting the *right* voice entry point
Desktop has two “voice-ish” behaviors:
- **Dictation-style input** (boring, not what you want)
- **Advanced Voice Mode** (full conversation)

Make sure you’re clicking the **headphone-style / voice mode icon**, not just a mic for dictation.

---

### 4. App version / feature rollout weirdness
Desktop features roll out unevenly. It’s not you, it’s… okay, it might still be you, but also this.

- Update the app
- Fully quit (not just close window)
- Reopen and try again

---

### 5. Network hiccups
Voice mode needs a stable connection. If it can’t handshake properly, it just refuses to start.

- If you click and nothing happens → suspect this

---

### 6. Plan / feature gating edge case
You *should* have it on Plus, but sometimes the desktop app lags behind mobile access.

---
