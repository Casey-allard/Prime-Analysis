**Quick take (so you can just buy):**
- **Best value, internal NAS:** **Toshiba N300 18TB** — reliable CMR, 7200 rpm, great price/performance for 24/7 Plex libraries.  
- **If you want max capacity + long warranty:** **Seagate IronWolf Pro 20TB** — 5-year warranty, data-recovery service, RV sensors; ideal in multi-bay enclosures.  
- **Cheapest per TB (if you’re willing to shuck):** **Seagate Expansion 20TB external** — often the lowest $/TB; remove the drive and use it internally or in a DAS/NAS.

**Buying notes for Plex:**
- Prefer **CMR** (not SMR), **7200 rpm**, large cache (≥256–512 MB).  
- NAS/Enterprise lines (N300, IronWolf/Pro, Red Pro, Exos, MG09, WD Gold) handle vibration and 24/7 use better than desktop drives.  
- If you shuck, you may need a **SATA power 3.3 V pin workaround** on some models; also expect external warranty terms to differ.  
- Put the **Plex metadata DB on SSD**; use the HDDs for media. It keeps menus snappy while the big drive serves streams.  
- For multiple drives, use **ZFS (RAID-Z1/Z2)** or mdraid; ensure a good fan curve—hot helium drives throttle and get noisy.
