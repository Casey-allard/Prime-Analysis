##### Magnitude ( $r$ ) Breakdown: The Reciprocal Pull

In this system, the radius is the inverse of the distance between a prime and the one before it. We call this the "reciprocal gap."

* **Gap of 1:**  Results in  $r \= 1.0$  (The  **Outer Ring** )  
* **Gap of 2:**  Results in  $r \= 0.50$  
* **Gap of 4:**  Results in  $r \= 0.25$  (The  **Inner Disk** )**Key Concept:**  Because  $r \= 1/g$ , small gaps between primes push the coordinate toward the edge, while large gaps act like an "inward radial pull," snapping the coordinate closer to the center.

##### Angular Logic ( $\\theta$ ) & The Golden Ratio

To find the exact angle for a prime ( $p$ ), we use:  $$\\theta\_p \= 2\\pi (p / \\phi) \\mod 2\\pi$$**Insight: Why use**  **$\\phi**$  **?**  We use the  **Golden Ratio (**  **$\\phi \\approx 1.618**$  **)**  because it is an irrational anchor. It ensures that prime points are distributed without the bias of integer alignment. This prevents "boring spokes" and clustering, allowing any observed clusters to represent real, intrinsic properties of the primes rather than artifacts of our graph.*While these coordinates tell us where a single prime sits, the entire "math environment" actually levels up as we count higher.*

#### 5\. Quick-Start Glossary for Aspiring Explorers

* **Reciprocal Gap:**  The inverse of the distance between primes ( $1/g$ ); it determines the radius ( $r$ ).
