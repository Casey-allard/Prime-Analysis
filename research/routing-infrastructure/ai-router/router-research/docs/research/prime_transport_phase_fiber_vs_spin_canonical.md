## Purpose

This note records the current exact-layer relationship between:

- the static exact chart `j -> (b, phi, r)`
- finite-horizon spin as predictive compression
- visible threshold and first-splitting events

It is a canonical exact-layer note. It does not treat downstream quotient work
as primary.

## Exact Objects

The exact recursive system supplies two different but compatible descriptions.

First, there is the exact lower-complexity chart:

- `j -> (b, phi, r)`

Here `b` is the base phase, `phi` is the refinement fiber coordinate, and `r`
is wheel depth.

Second, there is the predictive compression object:

- `(b, spin_H)`

where `spin_H` records the length-`H` future admissibility word along the exact
transport orbit.

The chart `j -> (b, phi, r)` is static and exact. The state `(b, spin_H)` is
also exact, but it is dynamical and predictive rather than merely coordinatizing.

## Mechanism Picture

The current mechanism picture is:

1. Hidden refinement becomes active immediately at the exact recursive layer.
2. The first visible threshold is reached only when specific predictive classes
   actually branch.
3. Those branching classes are not randomly located in `(b, phi, r)`.
4. But the decisive branching event is still more accurately described at the
   predictive spin layer than by a simple local rule on the static chart.

This is why the current mechanism should be described as partially geometric and
partially genuinely dynamical.

The geometric part is the nonuniform placement of first splitters in the exact
phase-fiber chart.

The dynamical part is the extra predictive structure carried by spin that has
not yet been reduced to a simple chart condition.
