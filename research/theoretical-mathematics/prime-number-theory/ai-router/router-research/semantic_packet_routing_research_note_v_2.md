## Core Thesis
The current router may only be the first low-order expression of a richer geometric process.

A more complete version may require:
- recursively refined angular routing
- packetized semantic transport
- sequence/dependency coordinates for meaning reconstruction
- structured coexistence of unresolved routes
- convergence / closure operators that merge branches when context stabilizes

In this view, routing is not merely bucket selection.
It is the progressive opening, transport, preservation, and closure of geometric-semantic degrees of freedom.

---

## Current Working Picture
### Stage 1: Angular routing
Angular routes are the first part of the address system.
They are not the whole mechanism.

At this stage, geometry gives:
- coarse partition
- local orientation
- initial retrieval bias
- a way to separate alternatives without collapsing them immediately

### Stage 2: Tokenization and packetization
Tokens likely remain necessary.
But they are probably not the final transport object.

A stronger model is:
- tokens = payload atoms
- packets = transport units
- angular coordinates = the first routing scaffold
- sequence/dependency metadata = the reconstruction scaffold

This is closer to a semantic transport protocol than a naive write-back memory system.

### Stage 3: Recursive growth and division
The geometry may need true recursive growth from an origin-like condition.

The rough intuition is:
- time is not a sophisticated law first
- time is initially just ordered progression, like `1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + ...`
- in shorthand: `r++`
- accessible structure increases step by step

What changes with growth is not just radius, but the number of available angular freedoms and possible route configurations.

## Important Refinement: Not Just Primes
Earlier notes over-centered primes. This note is more careful.

### Safer claim
Primes may still act as irreducible anchors.
But the routing scaffold is probably not "just primes."

A more likely picture:
- primes = sparse irreducible angular anchors
- composites = nested multi-route structures
- recursive growth/division creates finer coordinate freedom between and around those anchors
- unresolved route families may coexist in quantum-like superposed form before convergence

So the router may use:
- prime-like anchors for irreducibility
- composite-like overlap for convergence and closure
- recursive angular division for multiscale addressability

## Why 4 Matters in the Intuition
A useful informal marker in this line of thought:

- small numbers do not yet produce much route richness
- `4` may be the first number where there are clearly more than two angular paths available in a meaningful way

This is not yet a theorem.
It is a heuristic pointer that route multiplicity may begin to matter only after simple irreducible structure has already been established.

That is one reason the model may need both:
- irreducible atomic anchors
- and richer composite route spaces

## Structured Coexistence
A major idea worth preserving:

Intelligence may depend on holding unresolved possibilities as **structured coexistence**, not immediate collapse.

In this framework:
- unresolved branches remain geometrically separated
- angular structure preserves their distinctness
- packets carry their local meaning and dependencies
- convergence happens only when context sufficiently stabilizes

This resembles a quantum-like picture at the architectural level:
- multiple route possibilities remain open
- collapse is delayed
- closure is earned by coherence rather than forced too early

This is an analogy / design intuition, not a literal claim about quantum mechanics.

## Convergence and Closure
Recursive routing alone is not enough.
The architecture likely also needs a way to **close divisions**.

Without closure, the system keeps opening finer distinctions but never synthesizes.

A mature router may therefore need:
- expansion operators
- convergence operators
- merge rules
- context-phase transitions

### Working interpretation
- primes / irreducible anchors help open and orient
- composites / multi-route states help merge and compress
- when routes naturally converge, a phase shift occurs
- the result is a new stable semantic object or centroid

This would make the router not just a branching mechanism, but a cognition-like open/close system.

## Packetized Semantic Transport
This note keeps the packet idea and makes it more central.

### Why packets likely matter
For long-horizon reasoning, memory, and multi-agent systems, naive write-back may be too lossy.

Packets allow:
- transport of structured meaning
- dependency preservation
- route-local metadata
- delayed closure
- acknowledgment-gated integration

### Candidate packet fields
Possible header/body structure:
- `packet_id`
- `stream_id`
- `sequence_index`
- `dependency_refs`
- `semantic_type`
- `scope_level`
- `source_anchor`
- `target_anchor`
- `coherence_hash`
- `ack_required`
- `validity_horizon`

### Important refinement
Sequence numbers are not only ordering.
They may act as **discrete semantic coordinates**.

That means sequence/dependency indices could help with:
- meaning association
- reassembly
- dependency closure
- plan reconstruction
- packet-family coherence
